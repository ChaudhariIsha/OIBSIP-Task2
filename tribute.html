Bal Gangadhar Tilak

A man who Fought for Indian Freedom

Chronology of Lokmanya Tilak

  • 1856: Born in Ratnagiri district, Chikhali
  • 1877: Obtained his Bachelor of Arts in first class in Mathematics from Deccan College of Pune
  • 1877: Left his M.A. course of study midway to join the LL.B course instead
  • 1879: He obtained his LL.B degree from Government Law College
  • 1880: Inspired by Vishnushastri Chiplunkar, he co-founded the New English school for secondary education with a few of his college friends
  • 1880-81: Tilak started two weeklies, Kesari and Maratha with Gopal Ganesh Agarkar as the first editor
  • 1890: Tilak joined the Indian National Congress. He opposed its moderate attitude, especially towards the fight for self-government.
  • 1908-1914: Tilak was sent to Mandalay. While in the prison he wrote the Gita Rahasya. Many copies of which were sold, and the money was donated for the Indian Independence movement
  • 1920: Died at 01 aug, 1920
